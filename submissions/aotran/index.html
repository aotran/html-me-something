Why are there pictures of dresses on this page?

I am getting married in October. As a girl, I never gave much thought to my wedding day. (Frankly, as an adult I haven't given much thought to it either.) The thought of walking down an aisle in a froofy white dress fills me with dread.

Luckily, I can forgo at least one part of the traditional western-style wedding since I happen to have been born a Chinese woman. (No need to cry about appropriation of another culture.)

The traditional Chinese dress is called a "cheongsam" or "qipao." It was first popularized in the 1920s. A cheongsam is distinguished by its mandarin collar and form-fitting. Typically, they are body length with leg slits on each side. They can be made with many different materials, with more traditional styles in silk and cheaper versions in polyester satin.

Silk brocade

Silk brocade is a fabric made of silk which has patterns woven into it. The patterns can be in different colors, though I prefer a more subtle look as shown in this example.
